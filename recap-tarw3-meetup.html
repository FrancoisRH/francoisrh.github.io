üìÉ Sell vs. test: who's interviewing who?
üé§ Alison Eastaway - Head of Talent Acquisition at Sqreen


"Great talent is always a seller‚Äôs market", Alison says.

  • Positive side of Paris: we got great engineering schools, and talent are loyals (3 years on average, versus 11 months in the bay area), and relatively affordable. It isn‚Äôt hard to stand out of the crowd for a Recruiter, it is easy to be a decent recruiter.
  • Negative side of Paris: startup / devtools market is relatively young. Lots of competition for the same profiles.

Being a candidate is not a job. There are different kinds of candidates: people looking urgently for a job; people who want to move on and will take their time; people waiting to be contacted for the very right opportunity.


Why spend so much time on the hiring processes?

  • Give candidates the information they need, to enthusiastically choose us
  • Gather the information we need, to enthusiastically choose candidates


What does a candidate need to know before joining?

  • What will I be doing everyday (job descripton)?
  • Who will I work for (manager, founder) and with (colleagues in/direct)?
  • What is the industry, market, vision (area of interest)?
  • How will this job get me closer to my personnal goals (growth, learning)?
  • Will it meet my needs (Maslow's hierarchy)?


"Startup fail to sell jobs to candidates because they sell the top of Maslow‚Äôs pyramid ‚Äì candidates have no use of a huge meaning in their job if they can‚Äôt pay their rent."


What do we need to know before hiring a candidate?

  • Can they do the job (skills, knowledge)?
  • Will they enjoy working with us and vice versa (team interactions, common values)?
  • Do they get the 'bigness' of our mission (area of interest)?
  • Will they help my company improve (level up, add new skills, perspectives)?
  • Can we meet their needs (Maslow's hierarchy)?


Rule of thumb @ Sqreen

  • Be aware of bias
  • several interactions on different days
  • Multiple interviews
  • Different ways of testing (Q&A, take-home test, practical on-the-spot test)
  • Gateways: steps are pass/fail


Process should be timely, intelligent, pleasant and defendable (you can explain why you refuse an application for 10 minutes).


How much time on selling, versus how much time on testing?

  • All sell: nope. People tend to ask no question
  • All test: nope. Like reacting on the stress over a hardcore phone call.

It takes some time to balance sell and test. Before the interview, ask yourself what the 3 topics are you want to explore. Prepare 3 questions that you can rephrase.

üìÉ Improve Hiring in Criteo's R&D with contributions from our engineers
üé§ Nicolas Truchi - Senior Engineer Manager at Criteo


Steve Jobs has a saying that A players hire A players; B players hire C players; and C players hire D players. It doesn't take long to get to Z players.

Tune your hiring process the right way, to select A players. Also, you don‚Äôt only need A players.

Challenge #1: keep the same expectations about the people we hire, as we grow

Challenge #2: there is a lot of competition on the market. You want your candidates to have a good experience because they will refer friends

Challenge #3: interview at scale. ~140 interviewers. 40+ skype interviews / week. 25+ on site interviews / week

What are we looking for in a candidate? Knowing if this person will be a top performer in the job. This is a prediction challenge.

Best predictors of actual job performance:

  • 29%: work sample test (replicate a real piece of work)
  • 26%: general cognitive ability test (IQ test, SAT‚Ä¶)
  • 26%: structured interviews
  • 14%: unstructured interviews

(source: Schmidt & Hunter analysis, 1998)

Criteo tries to have interviews rounds that make sense

Structured interviews: behavioral questions (tell me about a time when you had to‚Ä¶?) and situational questions (what would you do if‚Ä¶?)

  • Consistent across interviews
  • Reduced bias
  • Fair to candidates

Whiteboard interviews: to check if the person understand what they are doing and if they can go further, not only if they have the answer to your questions.

Scaling interviews

  • Individual contributors are interviewing, and they are interviewing often ‚Äì individual contributors tend to YOLO it, they have a hard time accepting the structured interview philosophy.
  • Managers may not interview their new hires ‚Äì this is something that is difficult to explain to managers.

Nate‚Äôs Team:

Performance is important to Nate because he worked in several organizations, all size. How do we quantify performance? Mostly checking ‚Äúoutput‚Äù. Some organization define performance as volume (how many people did you hired?), as speed (how fast did you recruit people?), and some as quality. Nate never found a company that combined these 3 aspects.
To Nate, it really comes down to the input. Empowerment, accountability and recognition.

  • More empowerment ==> volume
  • More accountability ==> speed
  • More recognition ==> quality

Nate joined Criteo in Singapore in 2016, it was crazy and chaotic. He experienced tremendous growth. The team needed to adopt a common language. They needed to leverage data to manage performance, and thus drive accountability. Finally, the team moved beyond core performance, thinking ‚Äòabove of beyond‚Äô.
